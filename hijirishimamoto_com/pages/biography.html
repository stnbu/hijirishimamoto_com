Biography

Hijiri Shimamoto was born in Okinawa, Japan and received a Bachelor of Music degree in Saxophone Performance from The University of North Carolina School of the Arts in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. She studied with Taimur Sullivan and James Kalyn and took master classes with Arno Bornkamp, James Houlik and The New Century Saxophone Quartet. She has been teaching and performing since 1996 in both Japan and The United States. Hijiri enjoys large ensembles and was the principal alto saxophonist for The Winston-Salem Community Band for 7 years. She is currently a member of San Jose Metropolitan Band.

She can give lessons in both English and Japanese.
